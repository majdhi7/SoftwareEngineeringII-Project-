🚀 The Capstone Project

Nusuk App: Dependable Permit Management

Domain: High-Availability Crowd Management & Security

Team Role: Systems Engineer

The Nusuk application architecture is designed to handle extreme burst traffic during Hajj and Umrah seasons. The system processes millions of permits under strict physical constraints. By employing offline cryptographic QR codes and distributed edge servers, we eliminated single points of failure (like 5G congestion at security gates). Dependability is life-critical; a failure in permit validation directly causes hazardous crowd bottlenecks, proving that reliability must be paired with safety engineering.

❓ What changed in how I think?

Before HIMMA, I thought software engineering meant simply writing functional code. Now I understand it means building evidence — every design decision must be justifiable, traceable, and provable. Real engineering bridges technical execution with professional accountability.

📈 Early V1 reflection

In the beginning, I viewed dependability metrics merely as definitions to memorize. Now in this V1 stage, I am learning to apply concepts like POFOD and the "Swiss Cheese" resilience model directly to physical, high-stakes infrastructure like the Nusuk App. That practical application is a massive leap in comprehension.

⚖️ Safety vs Reliability — My understanding

The realization that a system can perfectly follow its specification and still cause harm was eye-opening. Safety is freedom from harm; Reliability is conformance to specification. Writing negative "Shall Not" requirements is just as critical as defining what the software should do.

💼 What I will carry into my career

The burden of proof is on the engineer. As I pursue consulting in Cybersecurity GRC, the principle that "technology fails without human discipline" will guide my approach. I will never design or audit a critical system without demanding a verifiable Safety Case.
